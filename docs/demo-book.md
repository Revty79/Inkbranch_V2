## Premise

Meridian Gate, the harbor's central beacon, fails as evacuation begins. Mira Quill and Jonas Vale investigate sabotage across ledgers, signal towers, and council orders while deciding whether to expose or contain politically explosive truths.

## Perspectives

- `Mira Mapmaker` (`mira-mapmaker`):
  - Civilian-safety and pattern-first lens.
  - High reveal sensitivity and truth-seeking posture.
- `Jonas Watchman` (`jonas-watchman`):
  - Procedure and order-first lens.
  - Moderate reveal sensitivity and stability posture.

## Hidden truths and reveal pressure

Core hidden truths include:

- a sealed abbey ledger proving evacuee prioritization corruption
- a false-beacon sabotage chain within official systems
- a regency debt bargain that trades long-term autonomy for short-term stability

Reveal rules are milestone-gated so legality shifts as runtime progression advances.

## Milestone structure

The demo milestones span three arcs:

- `stability`: evacuation integrity and signal control
- `truth`: source attribution and ledger recovery
- `endgame`: public exposure vs controlled compromise

Milestones include early progression, mid-pressure escalation, reveal-linked advancement, and ending-readiness checkpoints.

## Ending structure

The seeded endings are intentionally distinct:

- `harbor-renewal` (positive): full exposure + reform path
- `sealed-compromise` (bittersweet): controlled concealment + stability path
- `blackwater-fall` (negative): failed containment under unresolved pressure

Each ending uses explicit milestone/reveal eligibility requirements rather than prose-only interpretation.
